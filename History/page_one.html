Sumeria

around 5000 BC, farmers settled on the fertile land of southern Mesopotamia(now Iraq) known as Sumer. This formed the world's first great civilisation.
Living along the river valleys of the Tigris and Euphrates Sumerian farmers were able to grow grain and other crops, enabling them to settle in one place.
Sumerians also traded food for metals with people far away as present day Pakistan and Afganistan. They dug a network of ditches and canals as drainage channels on their lands.

By 3000 BC a number of city-states had developed in Sumer, the largest of which was Ur, with a population of 40,000. The first known system of writing originated in Sumer.
Pictographic writing evolved into a series of simplified wedge-shaped signs formed using reed stalks on clay tablets.

Babylonia

Political powe in Mesopotamia eventually moved north to the city of Babylon in Akkad, so the entire plane became known as Babylonia.
The first great dynasty of Babylon lasted about 300 years from around 1894 BC, reaching the peak of its influence under King Hammurabi.
